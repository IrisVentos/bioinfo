### **Part 1: DNA Replication & Motif Finding**
Explores the biological mechanisms of genome replication and the computational methods to identify replication origins.

### Key Topics:
-Finding Origins of Replication: Identifying ori regions where DNA replication begins
-Pattern Matching: Locating specific DNA sequences within genomes
-Motif Finding: Discovering recurring patterns in DNA that serve as binding sites for proteins
-Planted Motif Search: Finding subtle patterns hidden across multiple DNA sequences

### Biological Context:
Transcription factors and their binding sites
Molecular clocks in bacterial genomes

Case study: Mycobacterium tuberculosis dormancy response
Investigating hypoxia-triggered dormancy
Identifying transcription factors involved in the dormancy regulon

Applications:
Understanding bacterial survival mechanisms
Drug target identification
Regulatory network analysis

### **Part 2: Genome Sequencing & Assembly**
Addresses the computational challenge of reconstructing complete genomes from fragmented DNA reads.

### Key Topics:
-DNA Sequencing: Determining the precise order of nucleotides in a genome
-Read Generation: Breaking genomes into smaller, overlapping fragments (reads)
-Sequence Assembly: Reconstructing the original genome by finding overlaps between reads
-Graph-Based Approaches: Using computational graph theory for genome assembly

### Computational Challenges:
Handling millions of short DNA reads
Resolving ambiguous overlaps
Dealing with sequencing errors
Managing repetitive genomic regions

Methods Covered:
Overlap-layout-consensus assembly
String reconstruction from read pairs
k-mer analysis
